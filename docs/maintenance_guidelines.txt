Maintenance Guideline Reference - Fleet Safety

1) Brake System Safety:
- If brake wear exceeds 80%, perform immediate inspection and replacement planning.
- Vehicles with uneven brake response must be removed from heavy duty routes until validated.

2) Engine Thermal Health:
- Continuous operation above 105C indicates potential cooling system inefficiency.
- Inspect coolant levels, radiator flow, and thermostat behavior before redeployment.

3) Lubrication and Oil Policy:
- Oil quality below 35% requires urgent oil and filter replacement.
- Delayed lubrication maintenance increases engine wear probability significantly.

4) Preventive Service Cadence:
- Standard fleet preventive checks every 120-180 days depending on load profile.
- Vehicles beyond 180 days since last service should be prioritized.

5) Battery Reliability:
- Battery health below 50% can affect startup and electrical subsystems.
- Below 30%, plan near-term replacement and electrical diagnostics.

6) Operational Safety Notice:
- AI recommendations support decision-making and do not replace certified mechanic judgment.
